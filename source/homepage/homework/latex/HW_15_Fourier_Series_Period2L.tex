{\bf Problem 2:} Let $f(x) = x^2$ on $-1 \le x \le 1$ and define $f(x)$ to have period 2 ($f(x) = f(x+2)$).  Show that the Fourier Series for $f(x)$ is given by:

$$
f(x) = \frac{1}{3} + \frac{4}{\pi^2} \sum \limits_{n=1}^{\infty} \frac{(-1)^n}{n^2}\cos(n \pi x) 
$$

\vspace{0.2cm}

{\bf Extra Credit:} Use the result of {\bf problem 2} to derive the summation identities 

$$
\sum \limits_{n=1}^{\infty} \frac{1}{n^2} = \frac{\pi^2}{6} \qquad \text{and} \qquad \sum \limits_{n=1}^{\infty} \frac{(-1)^n}{n^2} = -\frac{\pi^2}{12}
$$
