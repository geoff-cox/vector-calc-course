\UL{I. Introduction}

\vspace{0.2cm}

Lets begin by considering the $M$th order differential equation of the form:
\begin{align}
	y^{(M)} + a_{M-1}y^{(M-1)} + a_{M-2}y^{(M-2)} + \dots + a_{1}y' + a_0 y = f(t)
\end{align}
where $M \in \mathbf{N}$; the coefficients $a_0, a_1, a_2, ... , a_{M-2}, a_{M-1} \in \RE$ and has the solution $y$ (function of $t$).

\vspace{0.3cm}

\EX Examples of this type of an equation would be:

\vspace{0.2cm}

\begin{tabular}{p{4cm}cp{4.1cm}r}
	&$\D{y^{(5)} + 3y'' = \sin t}$ & & (2) \\
\end{tabular}

\vspace{0.2cm}

where \,\, $\D{M = 
\ON<2->{\B{\, 5 \,\,}}, a_4 = 
\ON<3->{\B{\, 0 \,\,}}, a_3 = 
\ON<4->{\B{\, 0 \,\,}}, a_2 = 
\ON<5->{\B{\, 3 \,\,}}, a_1 = 
\ON<6->{\B{\, 0 \,\,}} a_0 =
\ON<7->{\B{\, 0 \,\,}}, \text{ and } f(t) = 
\ON<8->{\B{\, \sin t \,\,}}}$

\vspace{0.2cm}

\begin{tabular}{p{2cm}cp{1.9cm}r}
	&$\D{3y''' + 6y'' -14y = 0 \quad \implies \quad 
	\ON<9->{\B{y''' + 2y'' - \frac{14}{3}y = 0}}}$ & & (3) \\
\end{tabular}

\vspace{0.2cm}

where \,\, $\D{M = 
\ON<10->{\B{\, 3 \,\,}}, a_2 = 
\ON<11->{\B{\, 2 \,\,}}, a_1 = 
\ON<12->{\B{\, 0 \,\,}} a_0 =
\ON<13->{\B{\, -\frac{14}{3} \,\,}}, \text{ and } f(t) = 
\ON<14->{\B{\, 0\,\,}}}$

\UL{Follow-up Question}: What does this tell us about solutions to the differential equation below? 
\begin{center}
	$\D{y^{(M)} + a_{M-1}y^{(M-1)} + a_{M-2}y^{(M-2)} + \dots + a_{1}y' + a_0 y = f(t)}$ \\
\end{center}

It says that the complete solution, $y(t)$, comes from the sum of solutions of the 2 equations below:

$
{\setlength{\extrarowheight}{-0.15cm}
\begin{array}{ccc}
	&&\\
	(1) & \D{y^{(M)} + a_{M-1}y^{(M-1)} + \dots + a_{1}y' + a_0 y = 
	\ON<2->{\B{\, 0}}} & (homogeneous)\\
	&&\\
	&&\\
	(2) & \D{y^{(M)} + a_{M-1}y^{(M-1)} + \dots + a_{1}y' + a_0 y = 
	\ON<3->{\B{\, f(t)}}} & (non-homogeneous)\\
\end{array}
}$

\begin{center}
{\setlength{\extrarowheight}{-0.1cm}
\begin{tabular}{|p{0.4cm}p{10.6cm}|}
	\hline
	\hline
	&\\
	\DEF & The solution of eqn (1) is called the 
	\ON<4->{\B{\, {\bf homogeneous solution} denoted $y_h$ \,\, }} and \\
	&\\
	& The solution of eqn (2) is called the 
	\ON<5->{\B{\, {\bf particular solution} denoted $y_p$ \,\, }} \\
    &\\
	\hline
	\hline
\end{tabular}}
\end{center}
\begin{center}
{\setlength{\extrarowheight}{-0.1cm}
\begin{tabular}{|p{0.4cm}p{10.6cm}|}
	\hline
	\hline
	&\\
	\THM & The general solution, $y$, of\\
	&\\
	\multicolumn{2}{|c|}{$\D{y^{(M)} + a_{M-1}y^{(M-1)} + a_{M-2}y^{(M-2)} + \dots + a_{1}y' + a_0 y = f(t)}$} \\
	&\\
	& is given by $y=
	\ON<6->{\B{y_h+y_p}}$ \\
	&\\
	\hline
	\hline
\end{tabular}}
\end{center}

{\setlength{\extrarowheight}{-0.15cm}
\begin{tabular}{p{0.5cm}p{11.0cm}}
	Notes: & (1) The strategy we used to ``guess" the form of the particular solution is known as the method of 
	\ON<7->{\B{``Undetermined Coefficients".}} \\
	&\\
	& (2) The form of $y_p$ we ``guess" should not contain a constant multiple of any 
	\ON<8->{\B{ function in $y_h$ }} \\
\end{tabular}}

\end{column}\begin{column}{0.0cm}\end{column}\end{columns}
\end{frame}

	\ON<13->{\B{Because $e^{2t}$ is already a term in $y_h$}} \\
	&\\
	Compute: & $
	\ON<14->{\B{y_p'(t) = Ae^{2t}+ 2Ate^{2t}}} $ \\
	&\\
	& $
	\ON<15->{\B{y_p''(t) = 2Ae^{2t}+ 2Ae^{2t}+ 4Ate^{2t} = 4Ae^{2t}+ 4Ate^{2t} }} $ \\
	&\\
	Plug: & $
	\ON<16->{\B{y_p''-4y_p=2e^{2t}}} $ \\
	&\\
	& $
	\ON<17->{\B{4Ae^{2t}+ 4Ate^{2t}-4(Ate^{2t})=2e^{2t}}} $ \\
	&\\
	Group: & $
	\ON<18->{\B{4Ae^{2t}=2e^{2t}}} $ \\
	&\\
	Solve: & $
	\ON<19->{\B{4A=2 \,\, }}
	\ON<20->{\B{\implies \,\, A=\frac{1}{2} \,\, }}
	\ON<21->{\B{\implies \,\, y_p(t) = \frac{1}{2}te^{3t} }}$ \\
\end{tabular}}

\vspace{0.6cm}

\end{column}\begin{column}{0.0cm}\end{column}\end{columns}
\end{frame}
