\UL{III. Surface Integrals}
\scriptsize

\vspace{0.2cm}

Let ${\bf F}$ be a vector field.

\vspace{0.7cm}

Line Integral \,\, - \,\, $C$ is a curve

\begin{center}
	$\D{\int \limits_{C} {\bf F}({\bf r}) \cdot d{\bf r} = 
	\ON<2->{\B{\int \limits_{a}^{b} {\bf F}(\RT) \cdot \RPT \, dt}}}$
\end{center}

\vspace{0.2cm}

Surface Integral \,\, - \,\, $S$ is a surface

\begin{center}
	$\D{\int \limits_{S} {\bf F}({\bf r}) \cdot d{\bf A} = 
	\ON<3->{\B{\iint \limits_{S} {\bf F} \cdot \only<4->{\OS{\B{ d{\bf A} }}}{\only<4->{\OB}{ {\bf n} \, dA }} =\,}} 
	\ON<5->{\B{\iint \limits_{S} {\bf F} \cdot \only<6->{\OS{\B{ {\bf n} }}}{\only<6->{\OB}{ \frac{{\bf N}}{\only<7->{\CAN}{|{\bf N}|}}\, }} \only<6->{\OS{\B{ dA }}}{\only<6->{\OB}{ \only<7->{\CAN}{|{\bf r}_u \times {\bf r}_v|} \, du\,dv }} \,=\,}} 
	\ON<8->{\B{\iint \limits_{R} {\bf F}({\bf r}(u,v)) \cdot {\bf N}(u,v) \, du \, dv}}}$
\end{center}

\vspace{0.3cm}

where \, ${\bf N} = {\bf r}_u \times {\bf r}_v$ \,  is the normal vector to the surface and \, ${\bf n} = \frac{{\bf N}}{|{\bf N}|}$ \, is the unit normal. $R$ is the region in the $u,v$-plane (parameter plane).

\vspace{1.8cm}

\end{column}\begin{column}{0.0cm}\end{column}\end{columns}
\end{frame}

\UL{IV. Flux}
\scriptsize

\vspace{0.4cm}

Flux is a very important concept when modeling physical phenomena.

\vspace{0.2cm}
	
Mathematically, it is given by the surface integral
	
\begin{center}
	\text{Flux through a surface } S = 
	\ON<2->{\B{$\FLUX$}}
\end{center}
Where ${\bf F}$ is a vector field and ${\bf n}$ is the unit normal to the surface.

\vspace{0.2cm}

When dealing with flow through a surface, the vector field is typically written as: 
\ON<3->{\B{${\bf F} = \rho {\bf v}$}}

\vspace{0.2cm}

where 
\ON<4->{\B{\,\,\, $\rho$ is the density \,\,\, }} of the flowing substance and 
\ON<5->{\B{\,\,\,${\bf v}$ is the velocity\,\,\,}} of the flow.

\begin{center}
	\includegraphics[scale = 0.2]{Figures/10_6_Fig17.png} \hspace{1.0cm} \includegraphics[scale = 0.2]{Figures/10_6_Fig16.png}
\end{center}

\end{column}\begin{column}{0.0cm}\end{column}\end{columns}
\end{frame}

For ${\bf r}(u,v)$, we need

\vspace{0.2cm}

\quad $

\ON<3->{\B{\,u}}$

\quad $

\ON<5->{\B{\,u^2}}
\ON<6->{\B{\quad \implies}} \quad {\bf r}(u,v) =
\ON<7->{\B{\, [u,u^2,v]}}$ \qquad where 
\ON<8->{\B{$0 \le u \le 2$ and $0 \le v \le 3$}}

\quad $
z =
\ON<4->{\B{\,v}}$

\vspace{0.2cm}
\hspace{1.3cm} $\D{
\implies \quad {\bf r}_u =
\ON<9->{\B{\, [1,2u,0]}}}$ \qquad 
and
\qquad $\D{
{\bf r}_v =
